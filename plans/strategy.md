# LittleJohn strategy — 2026-07-20 (rewrite: the three-layer stack)

Supersedes the analytics-first framing below the fold. Same landscape read, different
spine: we stop treating safety, analytics, or greed as *the* wedge and organize the
whole thing as **doorbell → house → insurance.**

## The stack (the whole strategy in three lines)
- **Doorbell (acquisition):** the terminal — the best *honest, RH-native* place to find
  winners. The concrete day-one reason to show up.
- **House (moat + identity + retention):** **community-owned economics** — the fees come
  back to the people who show up. This is what we build the identity on and the only
  part that can't be forked.
- **Insurance (survive the exploit):** radical transparency + progressive
  decentralization — so the day we get hit (not if), goodwill and ownership carry us.

Everything else — analytics, the rug-crusade, safety badges — is a *content/trust arm*
feeding the doorbell, not a headline of its own.

## Why community-owned economics is the wedge (not safety, not raw greed)
1. **It's greed-compatible.** "Don't get rugged" fights greed; "the fees come back to
   *you*" *is* a money pitch. We get the money-draw and the trust-brand from one
   mechanic. (Hyperliquid bought back $2B HYPE for holders; pump.fun creator rev-share;
   Aerodrome bribes-to-lockers — all built cults by *paying the people who show up.*)
2. **It's the only real moat.** A scanner forks in a weekend, a terminal in a month. A
   *community that owns the thing* doesn't fork — pump.fun's moat was mindshare +
   belonging, never the curve (forked 100×). Give-back is the flywheel that makes that.
3. **It's the exploit-insurance we actually need.** Mercenary degens flee an exploit and
   post the funeral; a community that *owns* you rallies. Goodwill is the only thing
   that survives a hack, and ownership banks it *before* you need it. GMGN structurally
   *can't* copy this — they're a company extracting fees; their users are customers, not
   owners.

## The honest gaps (do not paper over these)
- **Day-one acquisition is still unsolved by this.** Community economics is the house,
  not the doorbell: a % of ~$0 early volume = ~$0. It pays nothing and means nothing
  until people are already here. The *terminal* has to earn the first thousand on its
  own merits.
- **"Why switch from GMGN?"** is THE open bet. GMGN is entrenched on RH (verified). The
  answer can't be features (we lose that race) — it's **ownership** (you own the
  terminal you trade on; its fees come back to you, not to GMGN's treasury) + RH-native
  depth + honesty. Plausible, unproven.
- **"Community-owned" is claimed by everyone** (SLVR claims it; every memecoin claims
  it). It's only a moat if it's *visibly, boringly real*: transparent treasury, real
  distributions, us provably removing ourselves as the single point of failure. Doing it
  *for real* is the entire differentiation.
- **Securities risk sharpens** the moment we pay fee-share to holders (Howey
  profit-expectation) → to counsel before any live distribution.
- **Still bets on RH Chain** surviving the Sept gas-subsidy cliff. If the chain dies,
  none of this matters.

## Sequence
1. **Terminal = the doorbell.** Best honest RH-native terminal: wash-resistant trending,
   smart-money/holder intel, deployer reputation, *and add execution* (swap through us).
   Execution is what turns the currently read-only terminal into a fee source.
2. **Community-owned economics from day one** — the terminal's trade fee is the first
   give-back. Small early, but *real and visible*: route it back to the community
   transparently. This establishes the identity long before the pad exists.
3. **Transparency-as-insurance, from day one** — never market un-ruggable; publish the
   upgrade powers + who holds the keys (the 2-of-3 Safe, task #9); publish the take and
   where every cut goes; start visibly decentralizing. This *is* the brand.
4. **The pad + DEX = the scaled money engine** — fired on "the day," feeding the *same*
   community give-back at scale (the take → the band → Bounties, per
   `bounties-engine.md`). Not a separate product — the big turbine bolted onto the
   flywheel the terminal already started.

## The content/distribution arms (demoted from headliners to megaphones)
- **Rug-drama, not rug-safety** (ZachXBT playbook). People don't share safety; they
  share schadenfreude. Auto-posted rug exposés (TG + Twitter) pull *attention* because
  they're entertainment, and funnel it to the terminal. The trust-badge inside the
  terminal ("deployer rugged 4×") is *retention*, not the draw. `rug-crusade.md` stays,
  demoted to this role.
- **"State of RH Chain" analytics** — the subsidy-countdown + vitals dashboard as
  *shareable content* (OG stat-cards, embeddable widget, the daily founder-voice
  artifact), not the beachhead. Cheap, on-brand, and its "we track the real signal"
  hook is genuinely sharp.

## "The day" trigger — fire the full pad when ≥2 hit
- Sept gas-subsidy cliff survived on **organic** volume
- July 29 earnings show real on-chain **mainstream** activation
- Robinhood pushes the **main-app 28M base** on-chain (not just the geofenced Wallet)
- stock-token / RWA supply grows materially

## The distribution unlock (still live, still ours)
"Grow the chain" aligns with **Robinhood's own incentive** → ecosystem grants,
amplification, integration. Distribution you can't buy and no commodity pad gets. The
terminal + analytics being genuinely *good for RH Chain* is what earns it.

## Ruled out (with reasons)
- **Safety as the headline** — REAFFIRMED. Safety is a trust layer + a content channel
  (drama), never the hook. Lead with money (find winners) + ownership.
- **Terminal-first** — **REVERSED (2026-07-20).** It's back *on* as the doorbell. The old
  objection (compete on features vs GMGN → lose) still holds — so the wedge isn't
  features, it's **ownership**: a community-owned terminal, which GMGN can't be.
- **Analytics-first beachhead** — DEMOTED. Good content, not the front door (below fold).
- **"Be the Alchemy" (data/RPC infra)** — lose to Alchemy. Build the curated product
  layer on top, not the infra.
- **Aggregator (Jupiter model)** — no liquidity fragmentation to route across yet (RH
  consolidating on Uniswap). Revisit only if the chain fragments.

## Monetization reality (be honest)
Early money is thin: a **small terminal trade-fee** (the first give-back seed) +
**ecosystem grants** (near-term, via Robinhood-alignment). Real money is the **pad +
DEX take** at scale, on the day. All of it flows through the *same* community give-back
— that's the point. It won't print early; it builds the ownership moat that lets
everything after it print.

route; deployer-reputation + trust-badge; the rug-drama auto-post pipeline; DefiLlama/
Blockscout composition for the vitals dashboard.

(retro OG airdrop instead, per `rug-crusade.md`), aggregation.
